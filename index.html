02 How It Works

From registration to approval — in seven steps.

01

Register

Create your account as a Student, Faculty Mentor, or Admin. The Group Leader kicks things off.

02

Form Group

The Group Leader creates a group and invites members by enrollment number. Teams are locked on confirmation.

03

Pick Tech Stack

Select your development stack from an admin-approved list — ASP.NET, Python Flask, React, or whatever's approved this semester.

04

Select Mentor

Choose from available faculty mentors. Mentor capacity is capped to ensure quality guidance for every group.

05

Submit Proposal

Enter your project title, description, and keywords. The duplicate detection engine runs instantly — you'll know immediately if there's a conflict.

06

Faculty Review

Your assigned mentor independently reviews the proposal. They see similarity scores too — a second, unbiased check before approval.

07

Approved

Green light. Your project is locked in, keywords are tagged, and the database ensures no future group can submit the same concept in the same stack.

03 The Detection Engine

Four layers of defense against duplicate projects.

L1

Student Warning

At submission time, the system normalizes your title, extracts keywords, and runs Jaccard similarity scoring against every approved project in the same tech stack. If there's a potential match, you see an advisory warning before you even submit.

Advisory
L2

Faculty Review

Your mentor sees the same similarity scores independently. They approve, flag, or reject — a human checkpoint that catches what algorithms can't: conceptual overlap hidden behind different wording.

Human Check
L3

Keyword Tagging

Once approved, normalized keywords are stored in the database. Every future submission is checked against this ever-growing index. The real gatekeeper — works across semesters and sections.

Database
L4

Admin Audit

Admins have a bird's-eye view of every project across all groups and tech stacks. The final safety net — full override power and system-wide duplicate reports.

Final Net
04 Built For Everyone

Three roles. One unified platform.

Student

Group Leader & Members

  • Create or join a project group with enrolled classmates
  • Select a technology stack and faculty mentor
  • Submit proposals with real-time duplicate warnings
  • Track project status from submission to approval

Faculty Mentor

Independent Reviewer

  • Review proposals assigned to your mentee groups
  • See similarity scores and keyword overlap reports
  • Approve, flag for revision, or reject projects
  • Manage group capacity to maintain quality guidance

Admin

System Governor

  • Configure semesters, tech stacks, and mentor pools
  • View system-wide duplicate reports across all groups
  • Override decisions and resolve edge-case conflicts
  • Full audit log for accountability and compliance
